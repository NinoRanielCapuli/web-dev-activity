Train with Love, Not Fear

Training your dog can be fun and affordable. Use positive reinforcement: reward your dog with treats or even a simple “Good boy/girl!” when they follow a command. Start with basic ones like sit, stay, and come. In Filipino households, dogs often guard the gate—train them to bark only when needed, not at every tricycle passing by.

Pinoy-Friendly Tips

If you live in a barangay where stray dogs are common, leash training is a must for safe walks. Play-based training, like fetching slippers or waiting calmly before meals, works well with family dogs. Consistency is key—even 10 minutes daily builds good habits. Remember: patience and love go a long way in creating a well-behaved dog.
