Healthy Feeding Habits

Feeding your pet the right food is one of the most important responsibilities of ownership. Just like humans, pets require a balanced diet that includes proteins, carbohydrates, fats, vitamins, and minerals. Poor nutrition can lead to illnesses, weakness, and even shortened lifespans.

Always choose food appropriate for your pet‚Äôs age, size, and activity level. Puppies and kittens need more protein to support rapid growth, while older pets may benefit from lower-calorie diets to prevent obesity. Avoid giving human food that may be toxic, such as chocolate, onions, grapes, and excessive salty snacks.

Fresh water should always be available. If possible, feed your pets on a consistent schedule to help regulate digestion and behavior. Treats can be used as rewards, but they should only make up about 10% of your pet‚Äôs daily calorie intake. A good feeding routine ensures your pet stays strong, happy, and full of energy every day.
