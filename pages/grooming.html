Grooming Your Pet

Regular grooming is essential for your pet’s health and comfort. Brushing helps remove dirt, spread natural oils, and prevent mats in their fur. Long-haired breeds require more frequent brushing, while short-haired pets still benefit from weekly sessions. Bathing should be done occasionally, usually once a month or as needed, to keep the coat fresh without stripping natural oils.

Don’t forget nail care! Overgrown nails can cause pain and walking difficulties. Use proper pet nail clippers and take small trims to avoid hitting the quick. Ears and teeth should also be checked—clean ears prevent infections, and dental hygiene reduces the risk of gum disease. Grooming isn’t just about appearance; it’s an important part of keeping your pet healthy and happy.
