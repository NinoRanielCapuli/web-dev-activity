Why Adoption Matters

Adopting a pet is one of the most loving choices a person can make. Every year, thousands of cats and dogs end up in shelters, waiting for a family to welcome them home. By adopting, you are not only giving a homeless pet a second chance, but also helping reduce the problem of overpopulation.

Shelter pets come in all shapes, sizes, and personalities. Some are playful and energetic, while others are calm and affectionate. Most have already been vaccinated and spayed or neutered, making them ready for their forever home. Unlike buying from breeders or pet shops, adoption fees are usually minimal and go toward supporting the shelter.
